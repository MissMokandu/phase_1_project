Types

  1. SEDAN

    Comfortably sleek and great for city use,
    key aspects

    • Comfortable ride – especially for city and highway driving
    • Fuel efficiency – better than SUVs or trucks
    • Good trunk space – for everyday items or luggage
    • Lower center of gravity – provides better handling and stability
    Car Type Pros Cons
    Sedan Fuel-efficient, comfortable, affordable Low ground clearance, not ideal for rough roads
  2. SUV

    Spacious and versatile, ideal for family trips and rough roads.
    Key aspects:

    • High ground clearance – handles bumps and uneven roads
    • Ample cargo and passenger space – perfect for families
    • Powerful engine – suitable for towing or adventure
    • Available in 4WD – great for off-road driving
    Car Type Pros Cons
    SUV Spacious, powerful, family-friendly Less fuel-efficient, harder to park in cities
  3. HATCHBACK

    Compact and practical for urban use.
    Key aspects:

    • Easy to maneuver – great for tight parking spaces
    • Rear hatch opens wide – more loading convenience
    • Affordable – good choice for first-time buyers
    • Foldable back seats – expands storage space
    Car Type Pros Cons
    Hatchback Fuel-efficient, affordable, city-friendly Less space than SUVs, limited rear-seat room
  4. ELECTRIC CAR

    Silent, smart, and sustainable — a modern driving experience.
    Key aspects:

    • Zero emissions – eco-friendly and clean
    • Low running costs – no fuel, fewer moving parts
    • High-tech features – often comes with smart systems
    • Instant torque – fast acceleration
    Car Type Pros Cons
    Electric Eco-conscious, smooth, affordable to run Charging stations may be limited, higher initial cost

Tips

  • Know your needs: City driving? Family travel? Off-road? Start there.
  • Set a budget: Include fuel, insurance, repairs — not just the price tag.
  • Test drive: How it *feels* matters as much as features.
  • Research reviews: Real experiences help you avoid common regrets.
  • Check history (for used cars): Service records, mileage, and accident reports.
